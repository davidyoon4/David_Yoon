Hey there!

I'm David Yoon, a design engineer from Potomac, MD. As a senior at Northwestern University, I've been able to design my own major called Human Centered Product Design. This, in addition to my Psychology minor, has driven my interest in problems that lie at the intersection between design, technology, and human behavior. I love researching and empathizing with people to truly understand the core problems they have and how to attack them.

Outside of class, I’m passionate about Design For America – a club founded at Northwestern that focuses on utilizing the design process to help communities in the Chicago area – and The Garage, Northwestern’s startup incubator. I also love playing soccer with Northwestern’s club team.

About my process

Question.

My first step is to ask questions. The more questions I can ask, the more information I can gather, and the better I can understand the problem.

Empathize.

I practice the human-centered design process. By prioritizing user needs and behaviors, I'm better able to analyze key issues and make life easier for the user.

Apply.

I work to apply a multidisciplinary approach. I believe designing at the intersection of technology and psychology produces effective, impactful designs.
