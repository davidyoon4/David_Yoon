Putting The Social
Back In Social Media

Context

Instagram has recently become an addicting, superficial, numbers-obsessed game that many people play. During our time in Silicon Valley, a team of 4 students analyzed why this was happening – and how to fix it.
Timeline
Jan - Mar 2020
Roles
UX Researcher, UX Designer
Outcomes
2 new Instagram feature concepts, Medium article

Research

We conducted interviews on tech professionals, teens, and parents on their experiences with this tech giant in the technology hub of the country. Along with screen time reports, articles on gambling, and much more, we found these takeaways.
People do NOT like to feel left out of things
Some features are based on slot machines – designed to pull you in
Instagram can cause episodes of depression and anxiety.

Research – cont.

We were able to find insights like these through the following methods.

Interviewing employees from Apple, Facebook, startup founders
Student/teenage user interviews and testing
Reading past redesigns and articles about Instagram

Human Psychology Exploits

- FOMO (Fear of missing out)
- Our yearn for social validation
- Social comparison
- Desire for a certain 'aesthetic'
- Dopamine rush from refreshing the Explore page (slot machine)

Drawbacks of High Social Media Usage

- Depressive symptoms
- Anxiety
- Unproductivity & Guilt
- Physical deterioration
- Paradox of choices (decision fatigue)

Prototypes

We then moved on to ideating and creating low fidelity prototypes. Since we were redesigning Instagram and not overhauling it, we decided to make many of our prototypes by overlaying features on screenshots of the current Instagram app.

User Testing

After developing some initial prototypes, we interviewed some fellow social media addicts – the 23 other members in our Bay Area program cohort. They were able to provide feedback and criticism that pushed our designs forward.
